MODULE 02 // TIMELINE ARCHIVE

Scroll-Driven Deep Space Trajectory

SEPTEMBER 5, 1977

THE DEPARTURE

Voyager 1 blasts off from Launch Complex 41 at Cape Canaveral, Florida aboard a powerful Titan IIIE-Centaur rocket, beginning a grand tour of the outer solar system.

MARCH 5, 1979

JUPITER FLYBY

Approaching the massive gas giant, Voyager 1 reveals the volatile volcanic eruptions on Io (the first ever observed on another body), intricate storm bands, and fine dust rings.

NOVEMBER 12, 1980

SATURN SLINGSHOT

Sweeping close to Saturn, the probe analyzes its massive icy ring system and performs a hyper-close flyby of Titan to evaluate its thick hydrocarbon atmosphere, slinging itself upward out of the ecliptic plane.

FEBRUARY 14, 1990

THE PALE BLUE DOT

At a distance of 6 billion kilometers, Voyager 1 turns its camera back toward Earth to snap one final image. Our home planet appears as a single tiny pixel, suspended in a sunbeam.

DECEMBER 16, 2004

HELIOSHEATH ENTRY

Voyager 1 crosses the termination shock—where solar winds slow to subsonic speeds—entering the turbulent magnetic boundary layer known as the heliosheath.

AUGUST 25, 2012

INTERSTELLAR SPACE

Crossing the heliopause, Voyager 1 officially leaves the heliosphere and enters interstellar space. It is the first human object to sail in the sea between the stars.
