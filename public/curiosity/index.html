CHAPTER 01 // ORIGINS

WHY MARS?

Mars has captured human imagination for centuries. A cold, red desert world today, it was once a planet of oceans, warm atmospheres, and rushing freshwater lakes.

Curiosity was built to answer a singular, heavy question: Did the Red Planet ever possess the key environmental conditions to support simple microbial life?

CHAPTER 03 // DEPARTURE

LAUNCH TO MARS

On November 26, 2011, an Atlas V rocket ignited at Cape Canaveral Space Launch Complex 41. It blasted Curiosity out of Earth's gravitational pull at speeds exceeding 36,000 km/h.

Ahead lay 560 million kilometers of empty, radiation-lashed space. Curiosity slept inside its protective cruise shell, dreaming of Gale Crater.

CHAPTER 04 // ENTRY PROFILE

7 MINUTES OF TERROR

Because signal travel time from Mars to Earth takes 14 minutes, the landing was fully autonomous. The rover had to perform a complex sequence of decelerations entirely on its own.

Friction heated the heat shield to 1600°C. A massive supersonic parachute deployed at Mach 1.7, followed by the ignition of retro-rockets on the descent stage.

CHAPTER 05 // LANDING REPORT

TOUCHDOWN CONFIRMED

On August 6, 2012, the rocket-powered Sky Crane lowered Curiosity gently on three nylon ropes. Once its six wheels touched Mars, the tethers were severed, and the Crane flew away to crash-land at a safe distance.

Ancient Lake Beds

By drilling into the mudstones of Yellowknife Bay, Curiosity discovered clay minerals, sulfur, nitrogen, oxygen, phosphorus, and carbon—key ingredients for hosting microbial life. The pH of the water was neutral, suggesting a drinkable freshwater lake that existed for thousands of years.

CHAPTER 06 // SUNSET OVER GALE

STILL EXPLORING.

STILL DISCOVERING.

As the blue twilight sunset settles over Mount Sharp, Curiosity stands alone on the rust-colored landscape. In the sky above, Earth is visible only as a tiny, bright cyan pixel.

More than a decade after landing, the robot continues to climb, analyze, and transmit data, rewriting our understanding of Mars with every Sol.
